About Me

ABOUT ME

I have a knack for going against the grain and not doing what is traditionally expected. When I left my island home in Jamaica to attend college in rural Illinois, my family and friends thought I was insane. It would be the first time being away from my parents and my first winter. After conquering the Midwest I was intrigued by the south and moved to Louisiana for graduate school. Los Angeles marked the start my design career but my need for even more adventure brought me back to Illinois and have lived in Chicago ever since. Here's a map of my journey.

I have been fortunate to have lived several cities and have visited many others. It's really exciting for me to be immersed in a new place and experience the sights and the people. Chicago has captured my heart however and it appears my days of moving around have come to an end. I still try to travel as much as I can and my last trip was during the 4th of July weekend to Mount Rushmore. I became a US citizen last year so it seemed appropriate.


MY FAVORITE PLACES (OTHER THAN CHICAGO)

LOVER'S LEAP, JAMAICA

An amazing view paired with the legend that inspired the name makes this a favorite place to visit when I'm in home. It never gets old overlooking this dramatic cliff to the ocean.

MONTERREY, MEXICO

This breathtaking Mexican city was a delight to visit. Monterrey was the highlight of a roadtrip from Baton Rouge. It showcases buildings by one my favorite architects, Luis Barragan.

NEW ORLEANS, LA

I was in nearby Baton Rouge when Hurricane Katrina hit in 2005 and it's been great to see the city recover. My boyfriend and I visited for Mardi Gras 2012 and it was awesome trip!

KINGSTON, JAMAICA

Of course I had to include the place I grew up. Though it sometimes gets a bad rap, Kingston is actually a great city full of fun, excitment and many of my childhood memories.
